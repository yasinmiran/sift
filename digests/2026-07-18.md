**What matters today:** A day after independent benchmarks called Moonshot's Kimi K3 strong but not frontier-leading, [Moonshot's own AlphaSignal-run tests claim it beats Claude Fable at coding for 4.6x less money and GPT-5.5 for 55% less](https://alphasignal.ai/news/moonshot-ai-s-kimi-k3-beats-claude-at-coding-for-4-6x-less-money), reopening the question of whose numbers to trust, a question a separate independent benchmark answered differently by [crowning Claude Fable 5 the strongest performer on a fresh, unpublished NP-hard optimization problem against GPT-5.6 Sol](https://charlesazam.com/blog/fable-5-gpt-5-6-sol-goal/). Away from benchmarks, [LG monitors have reportedly been silently installing an ad-pushing app through Windows Update without consent for years, converting a bundled McAfee trial into a paid subscription](https://videocardz.com/newz/lg-monitors-silently-install-software-through-windows-update-without-user-consent). And Amazon spent the day apologizing after [a billing bug quoted some AWS customers as owing as much as ==$1.5 trillion==](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p20#a260717p20), while Apple opened [DOJ settlement talks over its antitrust suit](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p16#a260717p16) even as it [raised Apple Music's price](https://www.theverge.com/tech/967379/apple-music-price-increase).

## AI / LLMs

- [Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 debuted at #3 on DeepSWE, matching Claude Fable and GPT-5.6 Sol as the first open-weights model to reach frontier-level coding performance at 2.8 trillion parameters](https://alphasignal.ai/news/moonshot-ai-s-kimi-k3-beats-claude-at-coding-for-4-6x-less-money), and AlphaSignal's own benchmarking runs claim it beats Claude Fable at coding for 4.6x less money and GPT-5.5 for 55% less, plus a 79% score on a small 13-task Repair Bench; all three claims come from AlphaSignal-affiliated evaluations, a day after Artificial Analysis and LMArena's independent numbers found the model competitive but not class-leading, so treat the specific margins skeptically until someone outside Moonshot's orbit reruns them.
- [Charles Azam ran Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol head to head on a fresh, unpublished NP-hard optimization problem, with and without each model's native "/goal" planning mode](https://charlesazam.com/blog/fable-5-gpt-5-6-sol-goal/): Fable 5 came out "an absolute beast," producing the best and most consistent solutions of either model, while /goal proved no generic "try harder" switch, sometimes finding a better search path and sometimes just giving a bad idea more time to mature.
- [Thinking Machines' Inkling, built by Mira Murati's team, became the top open-weight model on both ARC-AGI benchmarks, scoring 79.5% on ARC-AGI-1 and 36.5% on ARC-AGI-2 at under $1 per task](https://alphasignal.ai/news/mira-murati-s-inkling-tops-every-open-weight-model-on-arc-agi-2), an ARC Prize-run result rather than a self-reported one.
- [Anthropic says Claude Fable 5 will roll into all Max and Team Premium plans at 50% of limits starting July 20, with Pro and Team Standard users kept on usage credits plus a one-time $100 credit](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p30#a260717p30), Anthropic citing demand for Fable that has been "challenging" to keep up with.
- [Kaiser nurses say AI tools and workplace surveillance software are making their jobs harder and patient care worse](https://localnewsmatters.org/2026/07/15/kaiser-nurses-say-ai-workplace-surveillance-are-making-their-jobs-and-patient-care-worse/), a frontline complaint that drew one of the day's largest Hacker News threads (more below).

## Devtools & Infra

- [Netflix detailed the in-house LLM serving stack it built instead of using hosted APIs: a unified Model Scoring Service running NVIDIA Triton, which it rebuilt on vLLM in 2025 after outgrowing TensorRT-LLM as its workload mix broadened past standard chat inference](https://netflixtechblog.com/in-house-llm-serving-at-netflix-a5a8e799ea2c?source=rss----2615bd06b42e---4), a concrete account of what running your own model infrastructure at scale actually requires.

## Security & Privacy

- [LG monitors have been silently installing an "LG Monitor App" through Windows Update without any consent prompt whenever an LG display is plugged into a Windows PC, going back to at least 2024](https://videocardz.com/newz/lg-monitors-silently-install-software-through-windows-update-without-user-consent), and testing found it repeatedly pushes a 30-day McAfee antivirus trial (on 31 of 32 consecutive boots in one test) that converts into a paid subscription; disabling it requires digging into Windows' device-metadata install settings, not an app menu.
- [Cloudflare deployed WAF rules protecting WordPress sites from a critical unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in the REST API and a related SQL injection bug](https://blog.cloudflare.com/wordpress-vulnerabilities/), coordinated with WordPress ahead of public disclosure; WordPress has shipped fixes (7.0.2, with backports to 6.9.5 and 6.8.6) and is forcing automatic updates, but sites not yet patched remain exposed.
- [Flock Safety ended its rollout of "Distress Detection," a feature that used its acoustic gunshot-detection microphones to also listen for human screaming](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/07/victory-flock-ends-rollout-audio-distress-detection-human-voices), reversing course after EFF and community pushback over the surveillance and eavesdropping-law concerns it raised.
- [The UK's AI Security Institute finds recent open-weight models now lag frontier closed models' cyber capabilities by 4 to 7 months, narrower than the 6 to 10 month gap that held through most of 2025](https://www.techmeme.com/260718/p9#a260718p9), a data point for judging how much runway closed-model safety advantages actually buy.

## Startups & Industry

- [Apple and the DOJ are in early discussions to settle the 2024 antitrust lawsuit accusing Apple of illegally protecting its iPhone monopoly](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p16#a260717p16), a separate legal track from Apple's ongoing trade-secrets dispute with ex-employees now at OpenAI, even as [Apple raises Apple Music's price, with the individual plan up $1 to $11.99 and some Apple One bundles also increasing](https://www.theverge.com/tech/967379/apple-music-price-increase), citing rising licensing costs.
- [Amazon apologized after a bug in AWS's "estimated billing computation subsystem" generated bills as high as $1.5 trillion for some customers](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p20#a260717p20), including one UK customer whose usual sub-£1 bill briefly read £5.8 billion.
- [Japan plans to buy 27,500 next-generation Nvidia Rubin chips to build a homegrown foundational AI model for robots, in a Noetra-led effort joined by SoftBank, Sony, and NEC](https://www.techmeme.com/260718/p1#a260718p1), a state-backed bid to keep robotics AI infrastructure domestic.
- [China's National AI Industry Investment Fund gained voting rights in DeepSeek by joining its $7.4B funding round, while other investors like Tencent and JD got none](https://www.techmeme.com/260718/p3#a260718p3), a state-linked fund buying itself a say inside China's highest-profile AI lab; separately, [China's National Data Administration says the country's daily AI token consumption hit 140 trillion in March, up from 100 billion in early 2024](https://www.techmeme.com/260718/p6#a260718p6), a roughly 1,400x rise in a little over two years.
- [OpenRouter has discussed a potential sale to a larger tech company at a valuation above its $1.3B mark from May](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p19#a260717p19), the model-routing platform's first reported acquisition talks.
- AI infrastructure money kept flowing on both ends of the stack: chip startup Etched is [raising at a ~==$20B== valuation, and in a separate round led by Sequoia at $10B](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p27#a260717p27), while [Valar Atomics, which builds small nuclear reactors to power data centers, is in talks to raise $1B at a ~$5B pre-money valuation](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p22#a260717p22), the same kind of buildout [grassroots group HumansFirst organized protests against across 125 US locations today, calling it "unaccountable"](https://www.techmeme.com/260718/p11#a260718p11).
- [The Trump administration is reportedly weighing an independent regulator to vet AI model safety, one that would report to the SEC rather than a new standalone agency](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p25#a260717p25), an early and still-informal step toward the federal AI oversight the US has so far mostly avoided.
