**What matters today:** A day after independent benchmarks called Moonshot's Kimi K3 strong but not frontier-leading, [Moonshot's own AlphaSignal-run tests claim it beats Claude Fable at coding for 4.6x less money and GPT-5.5 for 55% less](https://alphasignal.ai/news/moonshot-ai-s-kimi-k3-beats-claude-at-coding-for-4-6x-less-money), reopening the question of whose numbers to trust. [Apple and the DOJ opened early settlement talks over the 2024 antitrust lawsuit alleging Apple broke the law](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p16#a260717p16), even as Apple raises Apple Music's price citing licensing costs. And Amazon spent the day apologizing after [a billing bug quoted some AWS customers as owing as much as ==$1.5 trillion==](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p20#a260717p20).

## AI / LLMs

- [Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 debuted at #3 on DeepSWE, matching Claude Fable and GPT-5.6 Sol as the first open-weights model to reach frontier-level coding performance at 2.8 trillion parameters](https://alphasignal.ai/news/moonshot-ai-s-kimi-k3-beats-claude-at-coding-for-4-6x-less-money), and AlphaSignal's own benchmarking runs claim it beats Claude Fable at coding for 4.6x less money and GPT-5.5 for 55% less, plus a 79% score on a small 13-task Repair Bench; all three claims come from AlphaSignal-affiliated evaluations, a day after Artificial Analysis and LMArena's independent numbers found the model competitive but not class-leading, so treat the specific margins skeptically until someone outside Moonshot's orbit reruns them.
- [Thinking Machines' Inkling, built by Mira Murati's team, became the top open-weight model on both ARC-AGI benchmarks, scoring 79.5% on ARC-AGI-1 and 36.5% on ARC-AGI-2 at under $1 per task](https://alphasignal.ai/news/mira-murati-s-inkling-tops-every-open-weight-model-on-arc-agi-2), an ARC Prize-run result rather than a self-reported one.
- [Anthropic says Claude Fable 5 will roll into all Max and Team Premium plans at 50% of limits starting July 20, with Pro and Team Standard users kept on usage credits plus a one-time $100 credit](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p30#a260717p30), Anthropic citing demand for Fable that has been "challenging" to keep up with.
- [Epoch AI found that style-mimicking AI text fools leading AI-writing detectors up to 29% of the time, with scientific writing the biggest blind spot at a 26% miss rate](https://alphasignal.ai/news/epoch-finds-ai-detectors-miss-26-of-scientific-writing-disguised-as-human), a data point against leaning on detectors for research-integrity checks.
- [Kaiser nurses say AI tools and workplace surveillance software are making their jobs harder and patient care worse](https://localnewsmatters.org/2026/07/15/kaiser-nurses-say-ai-workplace-surveillance-are-making-their-jobs-and-patient-care-worse/), a frontline complaint that drew one of the day's largest Hacker News threads (more below).

## Devtools & Infra

- [Netflix detailed the in-house LLM serving stack it built instead of using hosted APIs: a unified Model Scoring Service running NVIDIA Triton, which it rebuilt on vLLM in 2025 after outgrowing TensorRT-LLM as its workload mix broadened past standard chat inference](https://netflixtechblog.com/in-house-llm-serving-at-netflix-a5a8e799ea2c?source=rss----2615bd06b42e---4), a concrete account of what running your own model infrastructure at scale actually requires.
- [Vercel Sandbox no longer bills for data downloaded from the internet](https://vercel.com/changelog/data-downloaded-by-vercel-sandbox-is-now-free): installing packages, cloning repos, and pulling datasets are now free, though traffic to a sandbox's exposed ports and outbound traffic it sends stay billable.

## Security & Privacy

- [Cloudflare deployed WAF rules protecting WordPress sites from a critical unauthenticated remote-code-execution flaw in the REST API and a related SQL injection bug](https://blog.cloudflare.com/wordpress-vulnerabilities/), coordinated with WordPress ahead of public disclosure; WordPress has shipped fixes (7.0.2, with backports to 6.9.5 and 6.8.6) and is forcing automatic updates, but sites not yet patched remain exposed.
- [Flock Safety ended its rollout of "Distress Detection," a feature that used its acoustic gunshot-detection microphones to also listen for human screaming](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/07/victory-flock-ends-rollout-audio-distress-detection-human-voices), reversing course after EFF and community pushback over the surveillance and eavesdropping-law concerns it raised.
- [Texas won a court order suspending the domain of a pornographic website for violating the state's age-verification law](https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/news/releases/attorney-general-ken-paxton-secures-landmark-legal-victory-lock-pornographic-website-domain-and), among the more aggressive state enforcement actions yet against a site outside the reach of ordinary takedown requests.

## Startups & Industry

- [Apple and the DOJ are in early discussions to settle the 2024 antitrust lawsuit accusing Apple of illegally protecting its iPhone monopoly](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p16#a260717p16), a separate legal track from Apple's ongoing trade-secrets dispute with ex-employees now at OpenAI.
- [Apple is raising Apple Music's price, with the individual plan up $1 to $11.99 and some Apple One bundles also increasing](https://www.theverge.com/tech/967379/apple-music-price-increase), citing rising licensing costs.
- [Amazon apologized after a bug in AWS's "estimated billing computation subsystem" generated bills as high as $1.5 trillion for some customers](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p20#a260717p20), including one UK customer whose usual sub-£1 bill briefly read £5.8 billion.
- [OpenRouter has discussed a potential sale to a larger tech company at a valuation above its $1.3B mark from May](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p19#a260717p19), the model-routing platform's first reported acquisition talks.
- [AI chip startup Etched is separately raising funds at a ~==$20B== valuation and, in a second round led by Sequoia, at $10B](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p27#a260717p27), two simultaneous raises at very different valuations for the same company.
- [Valar Atomics, which builds small nuclear reactors to power data centers, is in talks to raise $1B at a ~$5B pre-money valuation](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p22#a260717p22), the latest sign that data-center power demand is pulling money into nuclear specifically, not just chips.
- [The Trump administration is reportedly weighing an independent regulator to vet AI model safety, one that would report to the SEC rather than a new standalone agency](https://www.techmeme.com/260717/p25#a260717p25), an early and still-informal step toward the federal AI oversight the US has so far mostly avoided.
