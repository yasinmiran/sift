**What matters today:** OpenAI had its biggest launch day yet, splitting GPT-5.6 into three durable tiers (Sol, Terra, Luna) and pairing it with ChatGPT Work, an agent that turns a goal into finished docs, sheets, and sites, even as ==Artificial Analysis found the best AI agents still clear barely half of real office tasks== and Sol's flashiest wins came from OpenAI's own benchmarks. The same day, OpenAI absorbed two blows: product chief Fidji Simo is stepping down after a severe POTS flare-up, and news publishers led by the New York Times asked a judge to sanction the company over its copyright-case discovery conduct. Money kept pouring into the buildout regardless: US VC funding hit a record $412.7B in H1 (86% of it AI), SK Hynix raised the largest-ever foreign IPO on a US exchange, and the Fed tapped Marc Andreessen for a new task force on AI's economic impact, while the EU and US both moved to tighten rules on kids online.

## AI / LLMs

- [OpenAI split GPT-5.6 into three durable capability tiers instead of one model](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-6/): Sol for hard problems like coding and security research, Terra for high-volume business tasks, and Luna for fast, cheap everyday work, priced at $5/$30, $2.50/$15, and $1/$6 per million input/output tokens; Sol also became the first verified frontier model to beat a full ARC-AGI-3 game, scoring 7.8% against Opus 4.8's prior-best 1.5%, and GPT-5.6 is now the default model behind Microsoft 365 Copilot and GitHub Copilot.
- [OpenAI launched ChatGPT Work, a GPT-5.6 agent that turns a goal into finished sheets, slides, docs, and web apps](https://openai.com/index/chatgpt-for-your-most-ambitious-work/), staying on multi-step projects for hours and checking in for approval on key actions; it's rolling out today to Pro, Enterprise, and Edu plans, with Plus and Business following within days.
- Independent testing landed softer than OpenAI's launch framing: [product consultant Claire Vo's own benchmark had Sol beating Claude Fable 5 on prototyping and PRDs](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/gpt-56-sol-vs-claude-fable-why-openais) but still preferred Sonnet 5 for agentic voice work, while [Factory's Droid platform folded Sol, Terra, and Luna into its automatic task-routing](https://alphasignal.ai/news/factory-s-droid-now-runs-gpt-5-6-sol-beating-claude-by-13-points) rather than crowning a single winner.
- [Artificial Analysis launched an independent leaderboard for ServiceNow's enterprise-agent benchmark](https://alphasignal.ai/news/artificial-analysis-reveals-top-ai-agents-fail-half-of-real-office-tasks) and found even the best model clears only 51% of real office tasks, a sober counterpoint to a day full of frontier-model victory laps.
- [Anthropic will start usage-based billing for Claude Fable 5 on July 12](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p33#a260709p33), pushing subscribers to pay per-token for its best consumer model while working to return it to subscription plans "when sufficient capacity allows," Wired reports, a sign of how tight frontier compute still is.
- [GLM 5.2 prepared a real UK small business's quarterly VAT return](https://toot-books.pages.dev/blog/glm-5-2-vat-benchmark), processing 59 transactions in 68 minutes for $2.73 in token costs and landing within 7 pence of the human-prepared figure, one of the more concrete "AI does real knowledge work" demonstrations yet from a Chinese open-weight model.
- [Cognition published a trustworthiness evaluation alongside SWE-1.7](https://alphasignal.ai/news/cognition-s-swe-1-7-strips-chinese-ai-bias-from-open-source-code-models), its coding model built on Moonshot's Kimi K2.7 Code base, finding the post-trained SWE-1.7 refused problematic requests the base Kimi model complied with, and scored comparably to US frontier labs on the same trust evals.
- [Google made AlphaEvolve, its Gemini-powered algorithm-discovery agent, generally available to everyone](https://alphasignal.ai/news/google-deepmind-opens-alphaevolve-to-everyone-after-doubling-klarna-s-ai) on the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform; Klarna used it to push one of its largest models' training throughput from 49 samples per second to 143, nearly tripling it.

## Devtools & Infra

- [pgrust, a from-scratch Rust reimplementation of Postgres, now passes all 46,000-plus queries in Postgres's own regression suite](https://github.com/malisper/pgrust) while staying disk-compatible with Postgres 18.3 installations; it's not production-ready or performance-tuned yet, but the goal is a multithreaded, connection-pooled Postgres without C's memory-safety baggage.
- [Zig creator Andrew Kelley published a pointed rebuttal to Bun's widely-shared Rust-rewrite writeup](https://andrewkelley.me/post/my-thoughts-bun-rust-rewrite.html), calling its claim that the old Zig codebase wasn't fuzzed "an outright fabrication" and arguing the binary-size and performance wins the Anthropic-backed team touted were reachable in Zig too, if compile times had gotten the same attention.
- [Ghostty and Zig's earlier departures from GitHub are becoming a small trend](https://www.howtogeek.com/why-developers-are-ditching-github-for-codeberg-and-self-hosting-alternatives/): Gentoo is now moving parts of its infrastructure to Codeberg too, citing 112 hours of GitHub downtime across 48 outages over the past year, even as GitHub itself keeps growing (36 million new developers in 2025).
- [Google shipped LiteRT.js, a high-performance runtime for running AI models client-side in the browser](https://alphasignal.ai/news/google-s-litert-js-runs-ai-models-in-browsers-up-to-60x-faster), claiming up to 60x speedups over CPU execution when a GPU or NPU is available via WebGPU or WebNN, and roughly 3x over existing browser AI runtimes on the same hardware.

## Security & Privacy

- [The EU Parliament extended the "Chat Control 1.0" CSAM-scanning derogation to 2028](https://www.patrick-breyer.de/en/eu-parliament-greenlights-chat-control-1-0-breyer-our-children-lose-out/) via a rushed pre-recess procedure: 314 MEPs voted against it, 47 short of the 361-vote absolute majority second-reading rules require to block it. Days earlier, [the US House passed the KIDS Act 267-117](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/07/house-passed-kids-act-senate-should-reject-it/), a package EFF says would force privacy-eroding age verification onto the whole internet; it now heads to an uncertain Senate vote.
- [The European Commission declined to extend the Digital Markets Act's interoperability mandate to social networks](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/07/european-commission-chooses-keep-eu-users-locked-behind-big-techs-gates/) in its first DMA review, citing "no clear demand," leaving Meta and TikTok free to keep blocking the cross-platform switching the law was meant to enable.
- [Following EFF's reporting on a Texas sheriff's office searching 83,000-plus license-plate-reader cameras](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/07/we-want-texans-know-their-rights-qa-mayday-health-impact-surveillance-abortion/) to track a woman suspected of self-managing an abortion, the nonprofit Mayday Health put up Houston billboards warning drivers they may be surveilled, expected to reach a million drivers over four weeks, a low-tech answer to a high-tech dragnet.
- [Microsoft patched RoguePlanet (CVE-2026-50656)](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/07/patch-for-windows-defender-0-day-could-allow-attackers-to-fill-hard-disk/), a Windows Defender zero-day that let remote attackers gain admin control even with real-time protection disabled; the same researcher who disclosed it in June has now sent Microsoft scrambling on several zero-days in a row.

## Startups & Industry

- [Fidji Simo is stepping down from her full-time role leading OpenAI's product and business work](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/963738/openai-fidji-simo-steps-down-ceo-advisor/) after a severe flare-up of Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome, a chronic condition she's had since 2019; she'll become a part-time adviser, with her responsibilities split between Greg Brockman, CFO Sarah Friar, and chief strategy officer Jason Kwon.
- [News organizations led by the New York Times asked a judge to sanction OpenAI](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/openai-faked-inability-to-search-training-data-hid-billions-of-logs-nyt-says/), alleging the company claimed for years it couldn't search its training data or chat logs while internally running a "Bloom filter" system called Project Giraffe and sitting on roughly ==78 million== de-identified ChatGPT conversations it used to gauge its own infringement exposure.
- [US venture funding hit a record $412.7B in H1 2026](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p34#a260709p34), up 30% on all of 2025, with AI startups pulling in $355.9B of that, 86% of every dollar; PitchBook warned the concentration leaves the market exposed if AI returns disappoint, since venture's power law means any gains would concentrate in a handful of winners anyway.
- [SK Hynix raised $26.5B in the largest-ever US IPO by a foreign company](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p41#a260709p41), pricing 177.9 million ADRs at $149 each in a book more than 7x oversubscribed and surpassing Alibaba's 2014 debut; the memory chipmaker plans to funnel proceeds into AI-memory capacity like its Yongin cluster.
- [Fed Chair Kevin Warsh named Marc Andreessen and Xbox CEO Asha Sharma to a task force studying AI's economic impact](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p36#a260709p36), alongside economist Charles Jones, on leave from Stanford to Anthropic's policy institute; all three are AI bulls like Warsh himself, and the task force is due to report by year-end.
- Capital kept finding new corners of the AI stack to fund: 23-year-old Mercor [acquired RL-environment builder Deeptune](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p39#a260709p39) three months after backing its $43M Series A, scraping-infrastructure startup [Oxylabs took its first outside money](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p46#a260709p46) at a $3.6B valuation from Warburg Pincus, and [Carlyle sold data-center power platform Copia to EQT](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p47#a260709p47) for $2.6B, a fivefold return underscoring how much private equity wants exposure to AI infrastructure right now.
- Humanoid robots closed in on human capability from two directions at once: [1X unveiled a tendon-driven hand for its Neo robot with 25 degrees of freedom](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p44#a260709p44), 22 in the fingers and palm plus 3 at the wrist, strong enough to lift a 20-pound kettlebell yet gentle enough to pick a grape off its stem; separately, [teleoperated humanoid robots removed gallbladders from live pigs in a UC San Diego preclinical trial](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/07/humanoid-robots-controlled-by-surgeons-did-world-first-operation-on-live-pigs/) published in Nature, with human surgeons piloting one and two robots working together on a second procedure, pitched as bringing surgical-robot capability to rural clinics at a fraction of the cost of dedicated systems.
- [Microsoft's carbon emissions rose 25% in 2025 to 34 million metric tons](https://www.theverge.com/tech/963728/microsoft-sustainability-report-2026/), which the company attributes mainly to data center expansion and its decision to stop buying "non-additional" renewable energy certificates, complicating its 2030 carbon-negative goal.

## Threads

- OpenAI ran a contradictory day: launching GPT-5.6 and ChatGPT Work with maximum bravado while, hours apart, losing product chief Fidji Simo to illness and getting hit with a sanctions push in the NYT copyright case, and Artificial Analysis quietly undercut the whole industry's agent-benchmark hype.
- A pattern keeps recurring: a lab ships a flashy self-reported benchmark win (Sol's ARC-AGI-3 score), and independent testers (Claire Vo, Factory, Artificial Analysis) land on a far more mixed verdict days later, the same dynamic that drove OpenAI to retract its own SWE-Bench Pro recommendation last week.
- Kids-online privacy took hits on both sides of the Atlantic in the same week: the EU's Chat Control extension and the US House's KIDS Act, both opposed by the same digital-rights groups, both advancing anyway over real floor opposition.
- Money chased AI infrastructure from every direction at once: SK Hynix's record IPO, a record VC half-year, the Carlyle-EQT Copia sale, Oxylabs' first outside raise, and the Fed's new Andreessen-led task force, the same week Microsoft disclosed a 25% jump in the emissions bill for its own buildout.
- Humanoid robots edged toward human-level capability in two unrelated domains on the same day, 1X's dexterous hands and UCSD's teleoperated surgeons, though both are still explicitly human-supervised rather than autonomous.
