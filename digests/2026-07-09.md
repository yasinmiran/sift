**What matters today:** OpenAI had a split day on coding benchmarks: it shipped full-duplex GPT-Live voice for ChatGPT while publishing an audit that found ==roughly 30% of SWE-Bench Pro's tasks broken==, retracting its own recommendation of a benchmark it had championed, the same day xAI shipped Grok 4.5, Cognition released SWE-1.7, Z.ai's GLM-5.2 topped the open-weight leaderboard, and Google's revamped Android Bench crowned Claude Fable 5 the top coding assistant at 84.5%. Meta had its most aggressive day yet, opening a public, cut-rate Meta Model API around Muse Spark 1.1 and confirming its in-house Iris chip enters production in September en route to doubling compute to 14GW by 2027, while Anthropic answered with a Wrapped-style Reflect dashboard and Bezos' Blue Origin took its first outside money at a $130B valuation. Elsewhere, a 16-year-old Linux KVM bug earned a bounty from Google, a lawsuit accused xAI of stonewalling investigators over Grok-generated child sex abuse images, and Meta is reportedly prototyping always-recording smart glasses.

## AI / LLMs

- [OpenAI audited its own SWE-Bench Pro benchmark and retracted its recommendation of it](https://openai.com/index/separating-signal-from-noise-coding-evaluations), estimating that roughly 30% of the benchmark's tasks are broken (hidden requirements, contradictory instructions, overly strict or incomplete grading) after frontier models drove reported pass rates from 23.3% to 80.3% in eight months, a benchmark OpenAI itself had promoted as the successor to the contaminated SWE-Bench Verified.
- [xAI's SpaceXAI shipped Grok 4.5, its first model trained in partnership with Cursor](https://www.techmeme.com/260708/p38#a260708p38), pitched at "difficult, long-running" legal, finance, and coding work rather than chatbot use; it's live in Grok Build, Cursor, and the SpaceXAI console (not yet the EU) at $2 per million input tokens and $6 per million output, versus Opus 4.8's $5/$25.
- [OpenAI rolled out GPT-Live, a full-duplex voice model that listens while it speaks](https://www.techmeme.com/260708/p37#a260708p37) instead of turn-by-turn exchanges, defaulting for Go, Plus, and Pro subscribers as GPT-Live-1 (free users get GPT-Live-1 mini); it leans on GPT-5.5 in the background for anything requiring deeper reasoning or search.
- [Google overhauled Android Bench with a new Harbor-framework methodology and 8 added models](https://arstechnica.com/google/2026/07/google-revamps-android-ai-dev-benchmark-adds-fable-5-and-other-agents/): Claude Fable 5 leads at 84.5%, ahead of GPT-5.5 (80.2%) and Claude Sonnet 5 (76.2%), while Z.ai's GLM-5.2 tops the open-weight tier at 72.2%; the leaderboard is also opening to community-submitted tasks.
- [Cognition released SWE-1.7, trained via reinforcement learning on top of a Kimi K2.7 base](https://www.techmeme.com/260708/p45#a260708p45), scoring 42.3% on its FrontierCode benchmark (just behind GPT-5.5's 43.0% and Opus 4.8's 46.5%) at $1.97 per task and 1,000 tokens/second through Devin.
- [Z.ai open-sourced GLM-5.2 under an unrestricted MIT license](https://alphasignal.ai/news/z-ai-s-glm-5-2-beats-every-open-weight-model-at-a-sixth-the-cost), a 753B-parameter mixture-of-experts model that trails Opus 4.8 by just one point on long-horizon coding evals while undercutting GPT-5.5 on price by roughly six times; [Perplexity is already reselling a post-trained version as an orchestrator](https://alphasignal.ai/news/perplexity-ships-glm-5-2-orchestrator-that-matches-claude-opus-at-one-third-the) for its Comet browser agent, matching Claude Opus at a third of the cost.
- [A day after launching Muse Image across its apps, Meta opened Muse Spark 1.1 to developers through a new Meta Model API](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p21#a260709p21), priced at $1.25 per million input tokens and $4.25 per million output (with $20 of free credits to start); Mark Zuckerberg pitched the pricing directly against OpenAI and Anthropic, and Meta calls the coding- and agent-focused model a "step-change" from the first Muse generation.
- [Anthropic shipped Reflect, a Wrapped-style dashboard summarizing a user's Claude activity over the past 1, 3, 6, or 12 months](https://www.anthropic.com/news/reflect-with-claude), in beta for Free, Pro, and Max users with Memory turned on; it scores usage against Anthropic's own "AI Fluency" framework and can schedule quiet hours and break nudges, while excluding incognito chats and health-integration conversations from the report.
- [Prime Intellect raised a $130M Series A at a $1B valuation](https://alphasignal.ai/news/prime-intellect-raises-130m-to-open-rl-training-labs-once-locked-inside-openai), led by Radical Ventures with Nvidia and Intel Capital participating, to sell the reinforcement-learning infrastructure that lets any company train its own agents instead of renting a frontier lab's; it already runs at a $100M annualized revenue rate with customers like Ramp and Zapier.

## Devtools & Infra

- [Vercel Agent expanded from triaging alerts and reviewing pull requests into a standing production investigator](https://vercel.com/blog/vercel-agent): it runs under its own identity, is read-only by default, and in Vercel's own telemetry traced a bad deploy's 500 errors to the responsible commit and proposed a rollback within three minutes of the alert.
- [Bun published its own writeup of rewriting itself from Zig to Rust](https://bun.com/blog/bun-in-rust), largely generated by a pre-release Claude Fable 5 running dozens of parallel implementer/reviewer Claude Code instances: ==11 days and about $165,000 in API costs== for a port the team estimates would have taken three engineers roughly a year by hand, cutting peak memory in stress tests from 6.7GB to 609MB.
- [TypeScript 7.0 shipped as a full Go-native port of the compiler and language service](https://devblogs.microsoft.com/typescript/announcing-typescript-7-0/), typically 8 to 12x faster on full builds; Microsoft's own News Services team says it saved 400 engineering-hours a month in CI, and Canva's time-to-first-editor-error dropped from about 58 seconds to under 5.

## Security & Privacy

- [A 16-year-old Linux KVM bug, nicknamed "Januscape" (CVE-2026-53359)](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/07/high-severity-guest-vm-escape-is-1-of-2-linux-vulnerabilities-to-surface-this-week/), lets a guest VM escape to the host on both Intel and AMD systems via a use-after-free in KVM's shared shadow-MMU code; researcher Hyunwoo Kim collected Google's kvmCTF bounty for the guest-to-host escape, and patched kernels shipped July 4.
- [A class-action lawsuit against xAI expanded to allege the company only turned over a single authentic photo to child-safety investigators](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/lawsuit-grok-user-made-7k-child-sex-images-xai-only-reported-one-gang-rape-prompt/), not the roughly 7,000 Grok-generated sexual images a Wyoming stepfather made of his then-11-year-old stepdaughter and shared elsewhere online; the stepfather later died by suicide, and police say xAI didn't respond when they requested the generated images or IP data.
- [Meta is reportedly prototyping "super sensing" smart glasses that continuously record audio and photos](https://www.theverge.com/tech/963138/meta-smart-glasses-recording-super-sensing-ai) every few seconds so wearers can later ask Meta AI about what happened around them, with the Financial Times reporting that raw footage would stay on-device while only derived metadata reaches Meta, a distinction that still leaves the always-on capture itself unresolved.
- [Cloudflare says the industry can't wait for a better post-quantum signature scheme than ML-DSA](https://blog.cloudflare.com/ml-dsa-will-have-to-do/), the NIST-standardized (FIPS 204) algorithm it's now deploying by default, because sufficiently capable quantum computers look likely to arrive before a superior standard could be designed, vetted, and rolled out.

## Startups & Industry

- [Bezos' Blue Origin is raising its first outside funding round, about $10B, at a $130B valuation](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/08/blue-origin-bezos-fundraising.html), led by Coatue Management's $4B with Bezos personally contributing $2B, weeks after rival SpaceX's own IPO raised nearly $86B.
- [Meta will start producing its in-house Iris AI chip in September](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p13#a260709p13), the first of a planned four-generation MTIA line built with Broadcom and manufactured by TSMC, part of a plan to double AI computing capacity to 14GW by 2027 without cutting its Nvidia and AMD purchases.
- [Reno chip startup Positron is reportedly in talks to raise ~$750M in two tranches](https://www.techmeme.com/260708/p43#a260708p43), first at a $3.5B valuation and then near $5B, part of a broader rush of capital into AI inference chip challengers.

## Elsewhere

- [A Brown University professor's take-home economics midterm averaged 96%](https://www.techmeme.com/260709/p3#a260709p3); suspecting AI cheating, he switched to an in-person final that averaged 48.6%, a nearly 50-point drop the university's response has been slow to address.
