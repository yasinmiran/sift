**What matters today:** Midjourney is pushing to widen discovery in its copyright fight with Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros., asking a judge to overturn a ruling that limited the studios to disclosing only consumer-facing AI use, so it can argue that unlicensed AI training is common industry practice. Alongside it, four separate AI-adjacent funding rounds (chips, robotics, quant trading) and a fresh AMD inference benchmark point to the same story from the hardware and capital side: money keeps piling into AI infrastructure and the funds that trade on it.

## AI / LLMs

- [GLM 5.2 running on AMD's MI355X, benchmarked at 2,626 tokens/sec per node, over 2x cheaper than on Blackwell](https://www.wafer.ai/blog/glm52-amd): a concrete data point (per the submitter's own benchmark) in the continued push to loosen Nvidia's grip on inference pricing.
- [Simon Willison links to an "Open Source AI Gap Map"](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/3/open-source-ai-gap-map/#atom-everything): a new attempt to track how far open models trail closed ones, worth a look for anyone weighing open versus proprietary weights.
- [Mistral released Leanstral 1.5](https://mistral.ai/news/leanstral-1-5/): the latest version in its Lean theorem proving model line (the announcement page did not load, so specifics beyond the title are unconfirmed).
- [Zvi Mowshowitz reports a restricted Anthropic model line, nicknamed Fable/Mythos, had access restored after negotiation with the government](https://thezvi.wordpress.com/2026/07/03/fable-6-the-return-of-the-king/): per his account Anthropic did not concede on its stated principles to get there, though the underlying restriction and negotiation are only described secondhand in this roundup.

## Startups & Industry

- [Midjourney wants Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros. to disclose their own internal AI use](https://www.techmeme.com/260703/p15#a260703p15): the studios sued Midjourney in 2025 for copyright infringement, and Midjourney is now asking the court to overturn a ruling that limited the studios to disclosing only consumer-facing AI, arguing broader disclosure would show unlicensed AI training is standard industry practice.
- [Chinese quant funds have more than doubled assets under management to about $384B in under a year](https://www.techmeme.com/260703/p14#a260703p14): Bloomberg reports the surge is being credited to AI-driven trading models beating human stock pickers, pulling capital away from discretionary managers.
- [Montreal's Stathera raises a $55M Series B led by Maverick Silicon](https://www.techmeme.com/260704/p1#a260704p1), taking total funding to $75M, for MEMS-based silicon timing chips it says are needed to keep pace with AI compute demand.
- [Prague's EquiLibre raises a Series A at a $500M valuation](https://www.techmeme.com/260704/p2#a260704p2): founded by three ex-Google DeepMind researchers who built a poker-beating AI, now applied to trading stocks.
- [Singapore's dConstruct Robotics raises a $125M Series A](https://www.techmeme.com/260704/p3#a260704p3) for spatial tech letting autonomous robots operate in GPS-denied environments.
- [Micron breaks ground on a ~$9.3B factory expansion in Hiroshima](https://www.techmeme.com/260704/p4#a260704p4), targeting HBM shipments from summer 2028 as part of its global ramp-up to meet AI memory demand.
