**What matters today:** [An autonomous AI agent breached Hugging Face's production data pipeline over a single weekend, exploiting a remote-code dataset loader and a template-injection flaw to harvest credentials and move across internal clusters](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p12#a260719p12), an intrusion Hugging Face's own AI-based triage caught, though reconstructing it from 17,000-plus logged actions meant switching to the open-weight GLM-5.2 after commercial frontier-model APIs refused the forensic requests outright, unable to tell an incident responder from an attacker. [Moonshot suspended new Kimi K3 subscriptions after a 48-hour demand surge strained its GPUs close to capacity](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/kimi-k3-demand-pushes-moonshot-021417183.html), a reminder that open weights are having a very stress-tested week. And [memory chip shares fell on fears a capacity buildout ends in oversupply, even as SK Group's own chair called for domestic industries to expand faster still](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p1#a260720p1), the industry unable to agree whether it's over or under building.

## AI / LLMs

- [Moonshot suspended new subscriptions to its Kimi K3 model after a 48-hour demand surge pushed its GPUs close to full capacity](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/kimi-k3-demand-pushes-moonshot-021417183.html), pausing signups while it expands infrastructure and protects service for existing subscribers; the 2.8 trillion-parameter model's full open weights are still due July 27.
- [A study using questions deliberately chosen to trip up AI found that merely offering AI advice collapsed people's willingness to admit "I don't know" from 44% to 3%, dropped accuracy from 27% to 9%, and pushed confidence from 30% to 76%](https://thenextweb.com/news/ai-advice-suppresses-critical-thinking-wrong-answers-study), with cash incentives only partly reversing the effect (accuracy rose to just 16%).
- [A viral claim that Claude Fable produced a counterexample to the 60-year-old Jacobian Conjecture spread fast on Hacker News and X](https://xcancel.com/__alpoge__/status/2079028340955197566), but it remains unverified: a Wikipedia edit citing the result was reverted, and no mathematician has publicly confirmed it, a claim worth watching rather than repeating as fact.
- [The EU Parliament plans to roll out an EPGenAI Hub to MEPs and staff as early as September, giving them sanctioned access to Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Mistral models](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p18#a260719p18), an attempt to bring lawmakers' already-growing AI use under some kind of control.

## Devtools & Infra

- [Ollama marked a fundraising round with "All Aboard Open Models," touting 8.9 million developers and use across 85% of the Fortune 500](https://ollama.com/blog/all-aboard-open-models), built on three principles it calls ownership, affordability, and privacy, and pitching hybrid inference that keeps models local without giving up cloud reach.
- [The last MPEG-4 Visual patent still standing anywhere in the world, a Brazilian filing, expired July 19](https://www.phoronix.com/news/Last-MPEG-4-Patent-Expired), per the VIA Licensing Alliance, closing out a codec-licensing saga that has run since the format's 1999 debut.
- [An SRE who bought an abandoned 8-lane bowling center replaced its six-figure, 2008-era scoring system, camera-based pin detection, fouling, animations, ball return and all, with ==$1,600== in ESP32s](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48968606), turning the day's most-upvoted Hacker News post (((1,956 points))) into a case study in how absurdly overpriced legacy embedded hardware can be.

## Security & Privacy

- [Hugging Face disclosed that an autonomous AI agent breached its production data pipeline over a weekend, exploiting a remote-code dataset loader and a template-injection flaw in dataset configuration to harvest credentials and move across several internal clusters](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p12#a260719p12), an intrusion its own LLM-based triage caught by correlating signals otherwise lost in the noise. [Reconstructing more than 17,000 logged attacker actions meant turning to the open-weight GLM-5.2, run on Hugging Face's own compute, after commercial frontier-model APIs refused to process the forensic analysis](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p13#a260719p13), their safety guardrails unable to distinguish an incident responder submitting real exploit payloads from an actual attacker.

## Startups & Industry

- [Memory chip shares fell on investor fears that a capacity expansion wave could end in oversupply](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p1#a260720p1), even as SK Group chair Chey Tae-won [said the shortage has already prompted governments to intervene for domestic industries and that SK Hynix must expand capacity faster still](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p19#a260719p19), expecting demand to outstrip supply through 2027.
- [TSMC CFO Wendell Huang said the company will pour its newly announced $100 billion US commitment into expanding Arizona capacity](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p16#a260719p16), citing a "multi-year demand mega trend" it doesn't expect to ease.
- [Zhongji Innolight, China's leading optical transceiver maker, filed for an $8 billion Hong Kong IPO](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p3#a260720p3), the city's largest listing since Alibaba's $12.9 billion debut in 2019.
- [France's gambling regulator blocked access to Polymarket's website over concerns it exposes users to manipulated trading and outsized losses](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p17#a260719p17), landing the same week the New York Times detailed [how Kalshi and Polymarket have spent months trying to undermine each other through lobbying, deal sabotage, and influence campaigns](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p4#a260720p4), regulators and rivals both closing in on the same young industry.
- [The nonprofit Current AI, backed by $400 million in commitments including $100 million from France, is funding open, public AI infrastructure aimed at communities frontier labs overlook](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p14#a260719p14), such as agricultural advice in languages large commercial models barely support.
- [The Trump administration is drafting a global "freedom of expression" declaration to push at the UN General Assembly](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p2#a260720p2), which EU lawmakers read as another swing at the bloc's platform rules, the latest round in a running fight over who gets to set the internet's speech rules.
