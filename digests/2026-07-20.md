## AI / LLMs

- [Alibaba's 2.4 trillion-parameter Qwen3.8 Max launched today, a day after its preview claimed it ranks second only to Fable 5](https://x.com/Alibaba_Qwen/status/2078759124914098291), the same day Moonshot's Kimi K3 kept its GPUs strained enough to force a signup pause; [The Verge framed the pair as China delivering a one-two punch to America's AI dominance](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/967781/chinese-ai-models-open-source-moonshot-kimi-k3-alibaba-qwen), which [prompted parts of the Trump administration to reignite plans for a de facto ban on foreign open-source models](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p17#a260720p17), even as [Bill Gurley argued in the Washington Post that Chinese open models are competition, not a threat](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p23#a260720p23), one story pulling policy three directions in a single day.
- [Anthropic mathematician Levent Alpöge posted on X that he and Claude Fable 5 found a counterexample disproving the 87-year-old Jacobian Conjecture](https://xcancel.com/__alpoge__/status/2079028340955197566), sketching the actual polynomial map himself rather than through an intermediary; [New Scientist covered the claim](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p28#a260720p28), but it has not been peer-reviewed and a Wikipedia edit citing it was reverted, so treat it as a named, specific claim awaiting verification, not settled math.
- [A study using questions deliberately chosen to trip up AI found that merely offering AI advice collapsed people's willingness to admit "I don't know" from 44% to 3%, dropped accuracy from 27% to 9%, and pushed confidence from 30% to 76%](https://thenextweb.com/news/ai-advice-suppresses-critical-thinking-wrong-answers-study), with cash incentives only partly reversing the effect (accuracy rose to just 16%).
## Devtools & Infra

- [Ollama marked a fundraising round with "All Aboard Open Models," touting 8.9 million developers and use across 85% of the Fortune 500](https://ollama.com/blog/all-aboard-open-models), built on three principles it calls ownership, affordability, and privacy, and pitching hybrid inference that keeps models local without giving up cloud reach.
- [An SRE who bought an abandoned 8-lane bowling center replaced its six-figure, 2008-era scoring system, camera-based pin detection, fouling, animations, ball return and all, with ==$1,600== in ESP32s](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48968606), turning the day's most-upvoted Hacker News post ((1,956 points)) into a case study in how absurdly overpriced legacy embedded hardware can be.
- [AMD plans to ship Helios, its first rack-scale AI system meant to rival Nvidia, later this year to Microsoft, Meta, OpenAI, and others](https://www.techmeme.com/260720/p20#a260720p20), at an estimated $5 million-plus per unit.
- [Airbus named French cloud provider Scaleway as the destination for roughly 900 applications, including ERP, manufacturing, and product-lifecycle systems, with an initial 70 given priority migration](https://www.theregister.com/columnists/2026/07/20/airbus-takes-flight-from-aws-what-happens-next-is-critical/5274109), citing the US CLOUD Act and hardening EU-US tensions, though it keeps its Skywise aviation-data platform on AWS.

## Security & Privacy

- [Hugging Face disclosed that an autonomous AI agent breached its production data pipeline over a weekend, exploiting a remote-code dataset loader and a template-injection flaw in dataset configuration to harvest credentials and move across several internal clusters](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p12#a260719p12), an intrusion its own LLM-based triage caught. [Reconstructing more than 17,000 logged attacker actions meant turning to the open-weight GLM-5.2, run on Hugging Face's own compute, after commercial frontier-model APIs refused to process the forensic analysis](https://www.techmeme.com/260719/p13#a260719p13), their safety guardrails unable to distinguish an incident responder submitting real exploit payloads from an actual attacker.
- [A hacker using the alias ByteToBreach wiped Romania's national land registry, ANCPI, using stolen valid credentials to destroy both live systems and backups after a failed extortion attempt](https://news.risky.biz/risky-bulletin-hacker-wipes-romanias-entire-land-registry-database/), grinding real estate transactions to a halt for over a week since notaries can't record sales and citizens can't get proof of ownership; the same actor breached Sweden's e-government portal earlier this year.
- [Bruce Schneier flagged a case where a single mistyped character in a Flock license-plate camera database led police to wrongly identify, track, and arrest a writer over stolen plates](https://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2026/07/on-flock-license-plate-tracking-cameras.html), the kind of small transcription error mass surveillance systems turn into a false arrest.
- [Researcher Adam Kues spent about ==$25== of GPT-5.6 compute time to find wp2shell, a pre-authentication WordPress core remote-code-execution chain that exploit brokers pay upward of $500,000 for](https://slcyber.io/research-center/exploit-brokers-pay-500000-for-a-wordpress-rce-i-found-one-with-gpt5-6/), reusing an OpenAI prompt originally built for a math conjecture and pointing it at WordPress's codebase instead; the flaw is now patched.
- [EDRi warned the EU is finalizing a deal to hand the US direct access to biometric and other sensitive traveler data in exchange for keeping visa-free travel](https://edri.org/our-work/the-eu-is-about-to-sell-our-most-sensitive-data-to-the-us-for-visa-free-travel/), an "Enhanced Border Security Partnership" civil society groups call irreversible once biometric data is compromised.
