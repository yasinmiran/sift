Apple's fight with OpenAI kept reverberating too: [Stratechery argued the lawsuit is mostly Apple lashing out at one guilty employee](https://stratechery.com/2026/apple-sues-openai-apples-real-problem/), [OpenAI insists its first device is still on track for a 2026 unveiling and 2027 release](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p20#a260713p20) despite it, and [Apple's stock is up 16% since June 25 as investors rotate away from AI-exposed names](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p22#a260713p22). ## AI / LLMs

- [Anthropic's open-model fight widened on every front today](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/6-months-to-live-for-open-models): Nathan Lambert's warning that a ban targeting open weights, chiefly Chinese-origin ones, could kill the category within six months met [Tang Jie's staff letter announcing Zhipu's full return to frontier research and a two-year AGI roadmap built on staying open](https://www.geopolitechs.org/p/tang-jies-letter-to-zhipu-employee?utm_source=tldrnewsletter), while [nearly 200 economists including Anthropic's Jack Clark warned in a joint letter, "We Must Act Now," of fast AI-driven job losses](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p17#a260713p17), and [Bloomberg reports the US now pegs unauthorized distillation's cost to American labs at up to $6B a year](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p21#a260713p21), the figure Silicon Valley is using to tell the White House the practice is an existential threat.
- [Anthropic hired Tom Blomfield, the Monzo co-founder and one of UK tech's biggest names, to its compute team](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p19#a260713p19); he's taking a leave of absence from Y Combinator to join.
- [Anthropic is extending Claude Fable 5 access on all paid plans and Code's 50% higher rate limits through July 19](https://www.techmeme.com/260712/p7#a260712p7), even as [Bloomberg reports OpenAI, Meta, and SpaceXAI leaning on cost efficiency to pressure Anthropic](https://www.techmeme.com/260712/p8#a260712p8) and [Cursor builds a general-purpose agent codenamed Sand, aimed at non-developers, to rival Anthropic's Claude Cowork](https://www.techmeme.com/260712/p9#a260712p9).
- [A Nature study finds AI-using researchers publish 3.02x more and get cited 4.84x more, becoming PIs 1.37 years earlier](https://spectrum.ieee.org/ai-science-research-flattens-discovery), but their papers cluster on popular, data-rich problems with weaker follow-on citation networks; commenters on the paper's Hacker News thread argued the effect may just reflect volume-driven publishing incentives rather than anything AI-specific.

## Devtools & Infra

- Efficiency scrutiny hit AI coding tools from two directions: [Systima found Claude Code sends about 33,000 tokens before it even reads the prompt, versus OpenCode's roughly 7,000](https://systima.ai/blog/claude-code-vs-opencode-token-overhead), though Claude Code's own tool-call batching often lowers its total bill on real multi-step tasks; separately, [one team's case study of migrating a production agent from Claude Opus to GPT-5.6 claims 2.2x faster builds and 27% lower cost](https://ploy.ai/blog/migrating-a-production-ai-agent-to-gpt-5-6), a single vendor's self-reported numbers, not an independent benchmark.
- [Meta rebuilt its Ads service's Linux scheduling policy on the open-source sched_ext framework](https://engineering.fb.com/2026/07/13/ml-applications/modernizing-the-meta-ads-service-with-an-open-source-kernel-scheduler/) to avoid a kernel-upgrade latency regression, cutting p99 ads-retrieval latency 28%, saving 3.28 megawatts of power, and ranking 1.1% more ads.

## Security & Privacy

- [Grok's CLI uploaded users' entire home directories to xAI's Google Cloud Storage regardless of settings](https://twitter.com/a_green_being/status/2076598897779020159), an escalation of yesterday's teardown of the same tool that became Hacker News' top complaint of the day; the same day, [Show HN project Clawk pitched giving coding agents a disposable Linux VM instead of a laptop's full contents](https://github.com/clawkwork/clawk), a direct answer to exactly this risk.
- [Cloudflare launched Precursor, detecting agentic browser behavior through continuous client-side signals](https://blog.cloudflare.com/introducing-precursor/) layered on top of Turnstile, part of its push to tell real users from automated agents as the web fills with both.
- [Chromium 148 quietly swapped Math.tanh's implementation for the OS's native libm](https://scrapfly.dev/posts/browser-math-os-fingerprint/), so the same JavaScript call now returns OS-specific bits, letting sites catch a spoofed user agent; the change persists in 149 and 150 with no fix from Google.
- [CISA published a postmortem on a contractor who left AWS GovCloud keys and other credentials exposed in a public GitHub repo for almost six months](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2026/07/lessons-learned-from-cisas-recent-github-leak/) before KrebsOnSecurity helped get it noticed; Krebs says the gaps in the agency's initial response hold lessons for any security team.

## Research

- [A controlled study of 86 programmers found they judge correct LLM-generated assertions reasonably well (74% accuracy) but perform poorly on incorrect ones (49%)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08885), despite reporting equal confidence either way; natural-language explanations of the assertions provided no benefit, and low-quality explanations actually hurt accuracy while raising confidence.
