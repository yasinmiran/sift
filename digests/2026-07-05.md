**What matters today:** A Guardian investigation found that the UK government's touted 20B pound "Stargate" data center at Cobalt, part of a jointly announced 30B pound AI investment with OpenAI and Nscale in 2025, looks like it was more hype than plan: neither company ever visited the site or filed planning applications. The EU Council separately pushed a fast-track "written procedure" to revive Chat Control 1.0, the voluntary messenger-scanning regime that lapsed in April, reopening Europe's fight over private-message surveillance. And Anthropic's Claude Fable model turned up independently in three hobbyist-scale builds today, an early sign it's doing real, unsupervised work in the wild.

## AI / LLMs

- Anthropic's Claude Fable model surfaced in three independent builds today: a [4D Gaussian splat format](https://adamraudonis.github.io/splats4D/) claiming 16-58x compression over raw splat data (HN has since flagged the post, with commenters disputing both the novelty and the benchmark math), a full [port of Command & Conquer: Generals to macOS, iPhone, and iPad](https://github.com/ammaarreshi/Generals-Mac-iOS-iPad/tree/main) that compiles the real 2003 engine natively for Apple Silicon via DXVK/MoltenVK rather than emulating it, and [sqlite-utils 4.0rc2](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/5/sqlite-utils-fable/#atom-everything), which Simon Willison says was mostly written by Fable for about (($149.25)): three hobbyist-scale projects, but a real signal the model is already shipping unsupervised work.
- [GitHub Copilot CLI now lets teams define custom AI agents in Markdown files](https://alphasignal.ai/news/github-copilot-cli-lets-teams-build-custom-ai-agents-from-markdown-files), turning one-off terminal prompts into repeatable, team-shareable workflows for security reviews, releases, and more.
- [A GitHub issue documents GPT-5.5 Codex's reasoning tokens clustering at exactly 516 tokens far more than other models](https://github.com/openai/codex/issues/30364): across 390,195 analyzed responses, 44% of its high-token replies land exactly at 516 (versus 1.3% for other models), a share that spiked from 0.11% in February to over 53% in May and coincides with degraded output on complex tasks, drawing heavy debate on Hacker News.
- [Armin Ronacher argues newer Anthropic models are getting worse, not better, at using non-standard tool schemas](https://lucumr.pocoo.org/2026/7/4/better-models-worse-tools/), because heavy post-training on Claude Code's flat edit-tool shape leaves Opus 4.8 and Sonnet 5 inventing made-up JSON keys against alternative schemas, failing up to 20% of the time in one user's session, a piece that drew attention on both Hacker News and Simon Willison's blog.

## Devtools & Infra

- [Cheyenne, Wyoming permanently revoked the discharge privileges of Goat Systems LLC, the contractor building Meta's roughly 800,000-square-foot data center](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/data-centers/cheyenne-suspends-data-center-fill-and-flush-and-closed-loop-discharges-after-meta-contractor-contaminated-its-reuse-water-system), after its fill-and-flush wastewater introduced the rare bacterium Cupriavidus gilardii into the city's reclaimed water system, a contamination found in February that took until late June to clean up and now bars all local data centers from similar discharges.
- [Iwo Kadziela, assisted by Codex, rendered a credible ASCII world map in just 445 bytes](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/4/building-a-world-map-with-only-500-bytes/#atom-everything) by deflate-compressing the coastline data and decoding it with a JavaScript snippet that fetches `data:` URIs, a neat data-compression curiosity via Simon Willison.
- [Shadcn/UI now defaults new projects to Base UI instead of Radix](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs/changelog): Base UI is built by the same team behind Radix "with everything they learned the first time," now stable at 1.6.0 with 6M+ weekly downloads, and shadcn rebuilt every component for both libraries so existing Radix-based projects and CI scripts keep working unchanged.

## Security & Privacy

- [A researcher disclosed a prompt-injection bug in YouTube Studio's "Ask Studio" assistant](https://javoriuski.com/post/youtube): malicious comments could make the AI leak private and unlisted video metadata via crafted links, and Google rejected the report as not a qualifying security bug, leaving it unpatched, the most discussed story on Hacker News today.
- [The EU Council pushed a fast-track "written procedure" to revive Chat Control 1.0](https://www.heise.de/en/news/Chat-Control-1-0-EU-Council-forces-messenger-scans-via-fast-track-11353659.html), the voluntary CSAM-scanning regime for messengers and email that lapsed on April 3 after Parliament refused a second extension, aiming to close the legal gap even as Google, Meta, Microsoft and Snap have kept scanning private messages without one, and to build pressure ahead of the separate, more contested mandatory scanning proposal known as Chat Control 2.0.
- [Chainalysis data cited by the Wall Street Journal](https://www.techmeme.com/260705/p7#a260705p7) puts crypto received by addresses tied to Iran, Russia and North Korea at over $100B in 2025, ==almost eight times 2024's total==, with Russia's ruble-pegged A7A5 stablecoin alone processing more than $93B for sanctioned entities and North Korea's hackers stealing over $2B, including the $1.5B Bybit hack.

## Research

- [A peer-reviewed ESO study by astronomer Olivier Hainaut](https://www.eso.org/public/news/eso2607/) warns that proposed satellite constellations, including SpaceX's planned million-satellite build-out and Reflect Orbital's 50,000 sun-mirroring satellites (each four times brighter than the full moon), would brighten the night sky three to four times and cut the VLT's field of view by up to 28%. Hainaut recommends capping the total at 100,000 satellites faint enough to stay below naked-eye visibility.
- [Texas A&M researchers led by Dr. Ashok Shetty](https://stories.tamu.edu/news/2026/04/14/scientists-reverse-brain-aging-with-a-nasal-spray/) show that two doses of a nasal spray delivering neural stem cell derived extracellular vesicles, carrying microRNAs that suppress the NLRP3 and cGAS-STING inflammation pathways, reduced brain inflammation, recharged neuronal mitochondria, and improved memory in aged animal models; the team has filed a US patent and published the results in the Journal of Extracellular Vesicles.
