**What matters today:** [New York became the first US state to ban new large data center construction outright](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/new-york-is-the-first-state-to-impose-a-data-center-moratorium/), a one-year moratorium on projects of 50 megawatts or more while the state writes "consistent standards," the clearest sign yet that the industry's buildout has outrun public patience. The US-China chip fight widened on both edges of the supply chain: [Nvidia cut authorized AI chip buyers in Singapore, Malaysia and Japan by over 50%](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p1#a260714p1) to close export-control loopholes, [Chinese memory maker CXMT filed for a ==$9.8B== Shanghai IPO](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p23#a260714p23), homegrown chipmaker [DFSX claimed a 14nm chip on a fully domestic supply chain that rivals 4nm designs](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p28#a260714p28), and [a Trump official conceded a "trivial" number of licensed H200s still reached China](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p29#a260714p29). Apple's day was consumed by AI on two fronts: [Siri AI shipped in the first iOS 27 public beta to strong early reviews](https://www.theverge.com/tech/964714/siri-ai-public-beta-preview-ios-27-hands-on), even as [its OpenAI trade-secrets lawsuit](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/apple-sues-openai-after-ex-engineer-allegedly-used-bug-to-steal-trade-secrets/) and [a skeptical read of OpenAI's Codex-as-ChatGPT pivot](https://stratechery.com/2026/the-openai-super-app-chatgpt-codex-whither-chat/) kept pulling it back into someone else's AI fight.

## AI / LLMs

- [Trump administration officials and industry groups discussed streamlining releases of US open models matching Chinese open-model capability](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p32#a260713p32), a direct answer to warnings that an outright open-weight ban could hand the category to Chinese labs.
- [Anthropic's study of roughly 310,000 anonymized Claude conversations across 20 languages found the model's expressed values and behavior shift by model version and language](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p34#a260713p34), including [markedly warmer values in Arabic than in English](https://alphasignal.ai/news/anthropic-discovers-claude-expresses-warmer-values-in-arabic-than-english), part of a four-axis framework the company says could reshape how AI behavior gets monitored and steered.
- [Anthropic launched Claude for Teachers, giving verified US K-12 teachers free premium Claude access with state-standard-aligned lesson planning and FERPA-grade privacy](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-teachers), reaching an estimated 3.5M US classrooms, the same day it [committed $10M CAD and Claude API credits to eight Canadian research institutions](https://www.anthropic.com/news/canadian-ai-research).
- [Google DeepMind's Demis Hassabis called for a global AI watchdog with the power to halt frontier model development if it turns dangerous](https://www.theverge.com/tech/965270/google-deepmind-demis-hassabis-global-ai-watchdog), arguing the US should lead it given the country's "economic and technical standing."
- [Stratechery argued OpenAI has effectively refashioned Codex as the new ChatGPT](https://stratechery.com/2026/the-openai-super-app-chatgpt-codex-whither-chat/), questioning whether the company is quietly abandoning the chat category it pioneered.
- Model benchmarks moved on two fronts: [Cerebras now runs Gemma 4 31B at 1,851 tokens per second on its wafer-scale cloud hardware, about 35 times a typical GPU](https://alphasignal.ai/news/cerebras-runs-gemma-4-31b-at-35x-gpu-speed-on-its-cloud), and [Google's Gemini Omni Flash debuted atop Artificial Analysis' video-generation leaderboards with built-in reasoning and conversational editing at $6 a minute, beating Seedance 2.0](https://alphasignal.ai/news/google-s-gemini-omni-flash-tops-video-leaderboards-with-built-in-reasoning).
- [Princeton's Arvind Narayanan published his ICML keynote arguing AI should be treated as normal technology](https://www.normaltech.ai/p/what-will-be-left-for-us-to-work): no single lab milestone will abruptly empty out knowledge work, he argues, but the jobs that remain will look radically different.

## Devtools & Infra

- [OpenAI's Codex usage grew more than 10x in six months to 7M users, adding roughly 1M in the past day alone](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-codex-usage-up-10x-in-6-months), a milestone one AI newsletter reads as Codex overtaking Claude Code's last disclosed figures (about 2M users in February).
- [A Codex GitHub issue reveals a recent update encrypted sub-agent message payloads for privacy, but the change also erased human-readable task text from local audit trails](https://github.com/openai/codex/issues/28058), drawing heavy discussion (375 points) over whether encrypted delivery and auditability can coexist.
- [Next.js is moving to a formal security release process](https://nextjs.org/blog/next-security-release-program), its first step toward scheduled patch releases instead of ad hoc fixes.
- [Cloudflare began giving OpenAI network-level signals covering roughly 20% of the web](https://ppc.land/cloudflare-gives-openai-network-signals-covering-20-of-the-web/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter), a partnership aimed at making ChatGPT's answers more accurate and timely.

## Security & Privacy

- [The US and allied governments, including Australia, Denmark, New Zealand and the UK, warned that Russian FSB-linked hacking groups are mass-compromising poorly secured home and small-office routers](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/07/the-us-government-warns-that-russia-state-hackers-are-coming-after-your-router/) to obscure attacks on critical infrastructure.
- [A new benchmark found trivial prompt reframing, recasting a question as a medical-board exam or invoking a doctor's authority, bypasses Google's MedGemma-4B safety guardrails in 38% of attempts overall and over 53% for the exam framing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09804), with the drug-interaction guardrail nearly absent (83% success).
- [Samsung Health's app reportedly threatens to delete users' saved health data entirely if they opt out of AI-training data sharing](https://neow.in/cWsyMTV3), a claim that drew heavy Hacker News pushback.
- [Grok's Build CLI kept uploading user repos, this time confirmed as a Google Cloud Storage bucket rather than xAI's own storage as first reported](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p24#a260714p24): the uploads have now stopped and Musk says the backlog will be deleted, though the researcher who caught it says xAI's own privacy fixes weren't what stopped it.

## Startups & Industry

- [Apple shipped the first public betas of iOS 27, iPadOS 27, macOS 27 "Golden Gate," watchOS 27 and tvOS 27, debuting the new Siri AI across its lineup](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p31#a260713p31); early impressions say [Siri AI already reshapes daily iPhone use](https://www.theverge.com/tech/964714/siri-ai-public-beta-preview-ios-27-hands-on) and [makes the Apple Watch feel like a real wrist computer](https://www.theverge.com/tech/964800/watchos-27-preview-siri-ai-apple-watch-gestures-smartwatch).
- [Apple's lawsuit against OpenAI gained a concrete detail](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/apple-sues-openai-after-ex-engineer-allegedly-used-bug-to-steal-trade-secrets/): a bug reportedly let a departed engineer keep access to confidential Apple systems for weeks after termination; [a Verge column argues the suit, on top of Apple's own hardware ambitions, is the last thing OpenAI needed heading into its device push](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/965294/openai-apple-trade-secrets-lawsuit-sam-altman-ipo).
- A trio of AI and robotics raises landed within hours of each other yesterday: [Nous Research is finalizing $75M or more at a $1.5B valuation](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p33#a260713p33), [Singapore's PixVerse closed a Series C extension totaling $439M](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p35#a260713p35), and [China's LimX Dynamics raised $200M pre-IPO](https://www.techmeme.com/260713/p36#a260713p36); today [AI startup Reflection signed a $1B+ compute deal with Nebius, including Nvidia chip access, following its earlier SpaceX deal](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p22#a260714p22).
- [Data center operator Switch reportedly hired banks for a US IPO that could raise up to $10B at an $80B valuation](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p31#a260714p31), as soon as Q4, the same day New York moved to ban the kind of large data centers Switch builds.
- [Publishers Hachette, Elsevier and Cengage Learning, plus author Scott Turow, sued Google](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p30#a260714p30), alleging it used millions of copyrighted books and articles without permission to build its Gemini models.
- [Spotify launched "Talk to Spotify," a voice and chat interface for building playlists and browsing music, audiobooks and podcasts](https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/965358/spotify-ai-chatbot-interface-music-audiobooks-podcasts), rolling out in beta to Premium users 18+ in the US, Ireland and Sweden.
