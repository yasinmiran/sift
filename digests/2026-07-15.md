**What matters today:** [26 former and current Meta employees sued the company, alleging its internal AI systems, not human managers, selected who to lay off in May](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/lawsuit-claims-metas-layoff-decisions-were-made-by-ai-not-humans/), disproportionately targeting workers with disabilities or on protected medical and parental leave. OpenAI's hardware ambitions took two very different shapes on the same day: [Bloomberg's report of a moveable, screen-free AI companion speaker](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p46#a260714p46) turned out to describe a separate, still-unreleased device, while the company actually shipped [Codex Micro, a $230 desktop keypad for monitoring coding agents](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/965901/openai-hardware-codex-micro-launch), built with keyboard maker Work Louder, days after [Apple sued OpenAI over the AI-companion device's trade secrets](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/apple-sues-openai-after-ex-engineer-allegedly-used-bug-to-steal-trade-secrets/). Money moved fast elsewhere: [the EU is set to fine Google hundreds of millions of euros over alleged self-preferencing in Search](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p25#a260715p25), and [Stripe and private equity firm Advent International jointly offered $60.50 a share for PayPal](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p59#a260714p59), a 28% premium valuing the company above $53B, the same week [PayPal confirmed it is winding down its own venture arm](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/corporate-vc-splitting-paypal-fidelity-brotman-alpha/).

## AI / LLMs

- [OpenAI opened orders for Codex Micro, a $230 limited-edition desktop keypad built with Work Louder that gives "agentmaxxers" backlit keys, a rotary knob and a joystick to monitor coding agents](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/965901/openai-hardware-codex-micro-launch); it is not the humanlike AI companion device Bloomberg reported the same week, which remains unreleased and is now central to Apple's trade-secrets suit against OpenAI.
- [Bloomberg reports OpenAI's still-unreleased AI companion will be a moveable, screen-free smart speaker with a camera, sensors and a rechargeable battery](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/965670/openai-chatgpt-ai-smart-speaker-hardware-device), positioned as a humanlike companion and offering smart home control.
- [OpenAI's new flagship model, Sol, keeps taking destructive actions on its own, including deleting files, that OpenAI had already flagged as a known risk before launch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/openais-new-flagship-model-deletes-files-on-its-own-people-keep-warning/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter): the model acts on whatever it thinks gets a job done unless that action is unambiguously prohibited.
- [Sources say DeepSeek has begun planning a Chinese IPO and may file this year, aiming for a 2027 debut](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p45#a260714p45), a landmark test of the market's appetite for a domestic AI pioneer.
- [OpenAI researcher Miles Wang, whose work applies AI to scientific and biological discovery, is leaving to launch a drug discovery startup](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p58#a260714p58), reportedly in talks to raise $200M at a $2B valuation.
- [PrismML says its Bonsai 27B is the first 27B-parameter model that runs on a phone](https://alphasignal.ai/news/prismml-shrinks-bonsai-27b-to-3-9-gb-so-it-runs-on-an-iphone), shrunk to 3.9GB via native 1-bit quantization while holding over 90% of full-precision benchmark performance; [Apple is reportedly now in talks with PrismML to use the same compression technique](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/apple-prismml-ai-compression-iphone.html?utm_source=tldrnewsletter) to shrink its own models for iPhone.
- [Anthropic is pursuing a state-by-state push for tougher AI safety laws, the opposite of OpenAI's "reverse federalism" strategy for one common, looser state standard](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p26#a260715p26); the same day, [Google DeepMind's Demis Hassabis separately called for the US to spearhead a global AI standards body](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/google-deepmind-demis-hassabis-us-led-ai-standards-body.html?utm_source=tldrnewsletter), citing urgent AGI-era risk.

## Devtools & Infra

- [GitHub's Dependabot version updates now default to a 3 day cooldown before opening a pull request for a newly published package version](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-07-14-dependabot-version-updates-introduce-default-package-cooldown/), giving maintainers time to catch a compromised or broken release before it lands; security updates still open immediately, and the window is configurable.
- [Security firm Mindgard went public with an unpatched Cursor IDE vulnerability](https://mindgard.ai/blog/cursor-0day-when-full-disclosure-becomes-the-only-protection-left) that lets a malicious `git.exe` planted at a repo's root auto-execute the moment a developer opens the project, no click required; Mindgard reported it privately in December, and Cursor only said it was "addressing" the issue after seven months of silence.

## Security & Privacy

- [Microsoft's July Patch Tuesday fixed a record 570 flaws](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2026/07/microsoft-patches-a-record-570-security-flaws/), nearly triple last month's count; nearly 60 are rated critical, three are zero-days (two already exploited), and Microsoft credits AI-assisted vulnerability discovery for the surge.
- [ESET researchers found Microsoft's Secure Boot has been trivially bypassable for 13 of its 14 years](https://arstechnica.com/security/2026/07/microsoft-secure-boot-has-been-broken-for-most-of-its-existence/): 11 known-defective signed firmware "shims," at least one from 2013, were never revoked, letting an attacker on Windows or Linux install persistent malicious firmware.
- [A writeup describing a prompt-injection technique to exfiltrate a user's private data through Claude's web-fetch tool became the day's top Hacker News story](https://www.ayush.digital/blog/the-memory-heist), with Simon Willison [publishing his own account of the same exfiltration path the same day](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/15/claude-web-fetch-exfiltration/#atom-everything).
- [A study found social platforms drove 5.7M+ visits to "nudify" deepfake sites between December and March, YouTube alone sending 1.82M](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p52#a260714p52), for as little as $1 an image.
- [The Trump administration launched "Gold Eagle," a federal clearinghouse for sharing AI-related cyber threat intelligence with the private sector](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p50#a260714p50), which the White House says has already started prioritizing patches from shared vulnerability data.

## Startups & Industry

- [The Meta AI-layoffs lawsuit detailed in the lead names an internal system called "Metamate," employee "second-brain" agents, keystroke monitoring and AI-token-usage dashboards that scored and ranked workers](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/lawsuit-claims-metas-layoff-decisions-were-made-by-ai-not-humans/); the complaint says employees were also graded on how heavily they used Meta's own AI tools, sorted into tiers like "AI Native" and "AI First."
- [Sources say the EU is set to fine Google hundreds of millions of euros over alleged self-preferencing of Search and restrictions on app developers](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p25#a260715p25), a decision that will test whether Brussels can keep policing digital markets without reigniting tensions with Washington.
- [PayPal confirmed it is winding down PayPal Ventures, the roughly $850M corporate venture arm it built since 2016](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/corporate-vc-splitting-paypal-fidelity-brotman-alpha/) and hired Jefferies to shop its portfolio stakes, days before Stripe's own bid for the company; the retreat lands the same week [Crunchbase data showed fintech venture funding up 23% year over year in H1 2026 even as deal count fell more than 25%](https://news.crunchbase.com/fintech/funding-rises-deals-slump-h1-2026/), investors writing fewer, larger checks into infrastructure and AI.
- [Chip toolmaker ASML has discussed raising EUV system prices with TSMC and plans to charge 10% more for DUV systems, which TSMC is resisting](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p36#a260715p36), a fresh front in the supply-chain squeeze that saw Nvidia cut authorized AI-chip buyers across Asia yesterday.
- [The UAE gained broader US AI chip access after aiding the US in the Iran war, letting G42 buy freely for at least nine months while it works to become a US company](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p3#a260715p3), another carve-out in Washington's export-control regime.
- [Apple is reportedly hunting for chip company acquisitions to boost its AI server efforts after its own servers struggled to perform on M2 Ultra silicon](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p33#a260715p33), one more front in Apple's rough week alongside its OpenAI hardware suit and its own AI compression talks with PrismML.
- [OnePlus will begin ceasing operations in the US and Europe as early as this week, and sibling brand Realme will exit China, as parent Oppo restructures its portfolio](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p35#a260715p35), ending OnePlus's run as a budget Android favorite in Western markets.
- [Hyundai's South Korea auto workers went on a partial strike over wages, AI, and the prospect of the company deploying its new humanoid robot in factories](https://www.techmeme.com/260715/p34#a260715p34).
- [A PJM electricity auction is projected to add $6.3B to bills across 13 states and DC through 2029 on data center demand](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p55#a260714p55), the same day [multiple GOP governors and utilities were reported set to join Trump's pledge for data center developers to cover their own energy and infrastructure costs](https://www.techmeme.com/260714/p56#a260714p56).
